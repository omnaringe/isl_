🇮🇳 What is Indian Sign Language?

Indian Sign Language (ISL) is the primary language used by the Deaf community in India — a complete, natural language with its own grammar, syntax and vocabulary, entirely distinct from spoken languages and other sign systems worldwide.

Officially recognized and standardized by the Government of India, ISL is used by an estimated 5–7 million Deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals across the country.

🔬 Detection Technology

Real AI Detection: The camera section uses MediaPipe Hands to detect and track 21 3D hand landmarks in real-time. Finger angles, tip distances, and curl ratios are computed and matched against ISL handshape rules for classification.

Improving Accuracy: For best results — good lighting, plain background, steady hand position, and holding each sign for 2–3 seconds. The 📌 Capture Sign button samples the current frame for highest confidence.

Admin Dataset: Admins can add, edit, and import custom sign entries. New entries appear live in the dictionary and text-to-sign converter.
